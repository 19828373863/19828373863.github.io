Just how good can China get at generative AI?
Its models may in time rival America’s. But that may not translate into an economic or military edge
May 9th 2023
这篇文章讨论了中美在技术领域，尤其是人工智能（AI）方面的竞争。虽然中国在某些衡量AI技术水平的指标上领先于美国，例如高度引用的AI论文数量，但它在建立基础模型方面落后于美国，而这些基础模型为生成型AI提供了能力。这是由于数据、硬件和专业知识的短缺，部分原因是美国对关键技术的出口管制。尽管中国正在努力解决其中一些挑战，但它可能难以充分利用生成型AI。最终，文章认为，无论是美国还是中国，都不太可能在AI建模方面建立不可逾越的领先地位，但美国可能在将尖端创新传播到整个经济中具有优势。本文仅供用来英语学习，观点仅供参考，在编辑时，编者已删除部分不和谐的内容。
IF YOU LISTEN to the bombast in Beijing and Washington, America and China are engaged in an all-out contest for technological supremacy. “Fundamentally, we believe that a select few technologies are set to play an outsized importance over the coming decade,” declared Jake Sullivan, President Joe Biden’s national security adviser, last September. In February ***, China’s *, echoed the sentiment, stating that “we urgently need to strengthen basic research and solve key technology problems” in order to “cope with international science and technology competition, achieve a high level of self-reliance and self-improvement”.
如果你倾听北京和华盛顿的夸张言辞，你会发现美国和中国正在全面竞争科技优势。去年9月份，乔·拜登总统的国家安全顾问杰克·沙利文宣布：“基本上，我们认为在未来十年里，只有少数几项技术将发挥超出比例的重要性。” 2月份，***重申这种观点，表示“我们迫切需要加强基础研究，解决关键技术问题”，以此来“应对国际科技竞争，实现更高水平的自力更生和自我提升”。
  1. Bombast: This word refers to language that is pompous, exaggerated, or pretentious. In this context, it refers to the exaggerated and grandiose claims made by leaders of both China and the United States regarding their countries’ technological capabilities.
  2. Technological supremacy: This phrase refers to the concept of one country being superior to others in terms of technological advancement, innovation, and dominance.
  3. “all-out” 是一个形容词短语，表示全力以赴、毫不保留地、尽一切可能的意思。在文中的上下文中，“all-out contest” 指的是中美两国之间在技术方面展开的激烈竞争，双方都在极力争取领先地位，并且投入了所有资源和能力。
  4. “a select few” 是一个短语，表示一些特定的人或事物中的选出来的少数精英。在文中的上下文中，“a select few technologies” 意思是指挑选出来的那些技术，这些技术被认为对于未来的发展和进步尤为重要。这些技术可能是高度先进、能够带来实质性成果、能够推动行业发展的关键技术。
  5. National security adviser: This term refers to a high-ranking government official who advises the president or other top officials on matters relating to national security.
  6. “Sentiment” 是一个名词，表示情感、态度或观点等。在文中的上下文中，“echoed the sentiment” 意思是指***发表讲话时表达了与美国国家安全顾问杰克·沙利文相似的观点或态度，即强调技术研究和基础研究的重要性，并强调要应对国际科学技术竞争的挑战，实现科技自立和自我提升的目标。
  7. Basic research: This refers to scientific research that seeks to expand knowledge and understanding of a particular subject without necessarily having a specific practical application.
  8. Self-reliance: This term refers to the ability of a country or individual to support and sustain themselves without relying on external assistance or resources.
  9. Self-improvement: This term refers to the process of making oneself better or more capable through personal effort and development.
No technology seems to obsess policymakers on both sides of the Pacific more right now than artificial intelligence (AI). The rapid improvements in the abilities of “generative” AIs like ChatGPT, which analyse the web’s worth of human text, images or sounds and can then create increasingly passable simulacrums, have only strengthened the obsession. If generative AI proves as transformational as its boosters claim, the technology could give those who wield it an economic and military edge in the 21st century’s chief geopolitical contest. Western and Chinese strategists already talk of an AI arms race. Can China win it?
目前，似乎没有任何技术比人工智能（AI）更让太平洋两岸的政策制定者着迷了。像ChatGPT这样“生成式”AI的快速进步，可以分析网络上的大量文本、图像或声音，然后创造出越来越接近真实的模拟品，这只会加强人们对它的痴迷。如果生成式AI证明像支持者所说的那样具有变革性，该技术可能在21世纪主要的地缘政治竞争中给那些运用它的人带来经济和军事优势。西方和中国的战略家已经开始谈论AI军备竞赛。中国能赢得这场竞赛吗？
  1. “worth of” 是一个短语，表示一定数量或价值的某种物品。在句中，“analyse the web’s worth of human text, images or sounds” 表示分析网络上包含了大量的人类文本、图像和声音，其总量或价值是巨大的。也可以换用“amount of”，意思相近，都表示数量或价值。
  2. “create increasingly passable simulacrums” 中的主语是“AIs”，动词是“create”，意为“创造”；“increasingly”则说明这些模拟品变得越来越好，能够越来越逼真地模拟出真实的物体、场景或人物。其中，“passable”表示“可通过”的意思，指生成式AI所创建的模拟品已经足够接近真实，以至于可以欺骗人类感官的检测和判断。而“simulacrums”则表示“模拟品”的意思，指生成式AI所创造出来的能够具有某种特定特征或品质的虚拟对象。
On some measures of AI prowess, the autocracy pulled ahead some time ago (see chart). China surpassed America in the share of highly cited AI papers in 2019; in 2021, 26% of AI conference publications globally came from China, compared with America’s share of 17%. Nine of the world’s top ten institutions, by volume of AI publications, are Chinese. According to one popular benchmark, so are the top five labs working on computer vision, a type of AI particularly useful to a***.
从一些衡量人工智能实力的标准来看，中国在某些方面早已领先（见图表）。例如，2019年中国在高被引用人工智能论文的占比上超过了美国；2021年，全球AI会议论文中有26%来自中国，而美国的份额为17%。世界排名前十的机构中，有九个是中国的，这些机构以发表AI论文数量而著名。另外，根据一个流行的基准测试，五个最顶尖的计算机视觉实验室也均位于中国，这种人工智能对于***尤为有用。
Yet when it comes to “foundation models”, which give generative AIs their wits, America is firmly in front (see charts 2 and 3). ChatGPT and the pioneering model behind it, the latest version of which is called GPT-4, are the brainchild of OpenAI, an American startup. A handful of other American firms, from small ones such as Anthropic or Stability AI to behemoths like Google, Meta and Microsoft (which part-owns OpenAI), have their own powerful systems. ERNIE, a Chinese rival to ChatGPT built by Baidu, China’s internet- search giant, is widely seen as less clever. Alibaba and Tencent, China’s mightiest tech titans, have yet to unveil their own generative AIs.
然而，当涉及到给生成式AI赋予智能的“基础模型”时，美国处于明显领先地位（见图表2和3）。ChatGPT及其背后的开创性模型（最新版本称为GPT-4）是美国初创公司OpenAI的杰作。除此之外，一些其他的美国公司，从小型企业如Anthropic或Stability AI到像Google、Meta和Microsoft这样的巨头（其中Microsoft是OpenAI的股东之一），都拥有自己强大的系统。中国搜索巨头百度打造的与ChatGPT竞争的ERNIE被广泛认为没有那么聪明。中国的科技巨头阿里巴巴和腾讯还没有推出自己的生成式AI。
  1. foundation models: 基础模型，指用于训练生成式AI的基本算法和模型结构。这些模型通常需要大量数据和计算资源进行训练，并且对于生成式AI的表现和效果至关重要。
  2. wits: 智能、机智，指生成式AI所具备的理解、分析和创造的能力。
  3. OpenAI: 一个人工智能研究公司，总部位于美国旧金山。该公司致力于研发最先进的人工智能技术，并通过开源软件和研究论文来促进人工智能领域的发展。
  4. Anthropic: 美国一家初创企业，主要从事人工智能和机器学习领域的研究和开发工作。
  5. behemoths: 庞然大物、巨头，是指在某个行业或领域中拥有强大影响力和实力的公司或组织。
  6. ERNIE: 一个由中国搜索引擎巨头百度开发的面向生成式AI领域的基础模型，被视为与ChatGPT竞争的对手之一。
  7. generative AIs: 生成式人工智能，是一种通过分析海量数据并自动生成新的内容和信息的人工智能算法和模型。这种人工智能可以用于自然语言处理、图像生成、音频合成等方面。
  8. “Brainchild” 是一个名词，指的是某个人或团队所创造、发明或提出的想法、计划或设计。在文章中，“brainchild of OpenAI” 指的是由美国初创公司OpenAI研发的生成式AI模型ChatGPT和其背后的基础模型。因为这些模型是由OpenAI的工程师们设计和开发的，所以被称为OpenAI的“brainchild”。
  9. “Brainchild” 是一个名词，指的是某个人或团队所创造、发明或提出的想法、计划或设计。在文章中，“brainchild of OpenAI” 指的是由美国初创公司OpenAI研发的生成式AI模型ChatGPT和其背后的基础模型。因为这些模型是由OpenAI的工程师们设计和开发的，所以被称为OpenAI的“brainchild”。
This leads those in the know to conclude that China is two or three years behind America in building foundation models. There are three reasons for this underperformance. The first concerns data. A centralised * should be able to marshal lots of it—the government was, for instance, able to hand over troves of * information on Chinese citizens to firms such as SenseTime or Megvii that, with the help of China’s leading computer-vision labs, then used it to develop top-notch facial-recognition systems.
据了解的人得出结论，中国在构建基础模型方面比美国落后了两到三年。这种表现欠佳有三个原因。第一个原因涉及数据。**能够动员大量数据 - 例如，中国政府能够将大量的 * 信息移交给SenseTime或Megvii等公司，然后再利用中国领先的计算机视觉实验室，开发出顶尖的面部识别系统。
  1. in the know: 指知情者或内行人，通常指那些对某个领域、行业或事件有深入了解的人。
  2. foundation models: 基础模型，指用于训练生成式AI的基本算法和模型结构。这些模型通常需要大量数据和计算资源进行训练，并且对于生成式AI的表现和效果至关重要。
  3. underperformance: 表现欠佳，不如预期或不如同类事物的平均水平。
  4. centralised: 集中管理的，指权力、资源、控制等在一个中心集中的状态或情况。与之相对的是分散化的（decentralized）。
  5. marshal: 集合、调动，指将人、资源、资金、信息等有组织地集合、调度和运用起来。
  6. troves: 大量、宝藏，指一大批、大规模的事物或信息。在语境中，它指中国政府向SenseTime或Megvii等公司提供了大量的监视信息，以协助这些公司开发面部识别系统。
  7. computer-vision: 计算机视觉，是一种使用计算机和算法来模拟和实现人类视觉能力的技术和方法，包括图像识别、目标跟踪、立体成像等。
  8. facial-recognition systems: 面部识别系统，是一种基于计算机视觉技术和算法的应用程序，用于从图片或视频中识别出人脸，并将其匹配到相应的身份信息上。
  9. Top-notch通常表示最高品质或卓越，常用来形容非常出色或令人印象深刻的事物。例如，一家顶级餐厅可能拥有美味的食物、无可挑剔的服务和良好的氛围。一位顶尖的运动员可能在自己的运动领域非常有才华和技能。
That advantage has proved less formidable in the context of generative AIs, because foundation models are trained on the voluminous unstructured data of the web. American model-builders benefit from the fact that 56% of all websites are in English, whereas just 1.5% are written in Chinese, according to data from W3Techs, an internet-research site. As Yiqin Fu of Stanford University points out, the Chinese interact with the internet primarily through mobile super-apps like WeChat and Weibo. These are “walled gardens”, so much of their content is not indexed on search engines. This makes that content harder for AI models to suck up. Lack of data may explain why Wu Dao 2.0, a model unveiled in 2021 by the Beijing Academy of Artificial Intelligence, a state-backed outfit, failed to make a splash despite its possibly being computationally more complex than GPT-4.
在生成式人工智能的背景下，这种优势在一定程度上没有那么强大，因为基础模型是在互联网上庞大的非结构化数据上进行训练的。根据互联网研究网站W3Techs的数据，美国的模型构建者受益于56%的网站都是用英语编写的，而只有1.5%的网站是用中文编写的。正如斯坦福大学的Yiqin Fu指出的那样，中国人主要通过微信和微博等移动超级应用程序与互联网互动。这些应用程序是“封闭的花园”，因此它们的很多内容没有被搜索引擎索引。这使得该内容对于AI模型来说更难吸收。缺乏数据可能可以解释为什么中国国家支持的北京人工智能科学院在2021年推出了计算复杂性可能比GPT-4更高的Wu Dao 2.0模型，但却没有引起轰动。
  1. formidable: 强大的，令人敬畏的
  2. generative AIs: 生成式人工智能，指那些可以自动生成数据、文本、图像等内容的人工智能。
  3. foundation models: 基础模型，指用于训练生成式AI的基本算法和模型结构。
  4. unstructured data: 非结构化数据，指没有预先定义好的数据模型或组织方式的数据，如文本、音频和视频等。
  5. W3Techs: 一个互联网研究网站。
  6. walled gardens: 封闭的花园，指类似于社交媒体应用程序等只允许在自己平台内部进行交流和分享的闭环生态系统。
  7. computationally: 计算上的，指与计算机处理相关的事物，如算法复杂度、运行速度等。
  8. state-backed outfit: 国有机构，指由政府支持或控制的组织或企业。
The second reason for China’s lacklustre generative achievements has to do with hardware. Last year America imposed export controls on technology that might give China a leg-up in AI. These cover the powerful microprocessors used in the cloud-computing data centres where foundation models do their learning, and the chipmaking tools that could enable China to build such semiconductors on its own.
中国在生成式人工智能方面成就欠佳的第二个原因与硬件有关。去年，美国对可能帮助中国在人工智能领域取得优势的技术实施了出口管制。这些技术包括用于云计算数据中心的强大微处理器，基础模型进行学习的场所以及可能使中国自行生产这种半导体的芯片制造工具。
  1. lacklustre: 意为”平淡无奇的、乏味的”，用于描述缺乏兴趣或激情的事物。
  2. generative: 意为”生成式的”，指的是通过对数据进行分析和学习来生成新的数据或内容的技术。
  3. export controls: 出口管制，指政府采取的限制某些敏感产品或技术出口的措施。
  4. microprocessors：微处理器，是一种集成电路，包含处理器核心和其他必要的组件，可执行计算机程序。
  5. cloud-computing: 云计算，是一种基于互联网的计算方式，将共享的计算资源和数据提供给多个设备。
  6. data centres: 数据中心，是一个集中的网络架构，用于存储、管理和传输大量的数据。
  7. chipmaking tools: 芯片制造工具，是指用于生产半导体芯片的设备和工具。
  8. semiconductors: 半导体，是指在电学特性上介于导体和绝缘体之间，可用于制造各种电子元件。
That hurt Chinese model-builders. An analysis of 26 big Chinese models by the Centre for the Governance of AI, a British think-tank, found that more than half depended on Nvidia, an American chip designer, for their processing power. Some reports suggest that SMIC, China’s biggest chipmaker, has produced prototypes just a generation or two behind TSMC, the Taiwanese industry leader that manufactures chips for Nvidia (see chart 4). But SMIC can probably mass- produce only chips which TSMC was churning out by the million three or four years ago.
Chinese AI firms are having trouble getting their hands on another American export: know-how. America remains a magnet for the world’s tech talent; two- thirds of AI experts in America who present papers at the main AI conference are foreign-born. Chinese engineers made up 27% of that select group in 2019. Many Chinese AI boffins studied or worked in America before bringing expertise back home. The covid-19 pandemic and rising Sino-American tensions are causing their numbers to dwindle. In the first half of 2022 America granted half as many visas to Chinese students as in the same period in 2019.
这对中国的模型构建者造成了困扰。英国智库“人工智能治理中心”对26个重要的中国模型进行的分析发现，超过一半的模型依赖于美国芯片设计公司Nvidia的处理能力。一些报告表明，中国最大的芯片制造商中芯国际有可能已经生产出比TSMC（为Nvidia制造芯片的台湾产业领袖）还落后一两代的原型机（见图4）。但是中芯国际很可能只能批量生产三四年前由TSMC生产的那些芯片。
中国的人工智能公司正在面临获取另一种美国出口的技术：专业知识的困境。美国仍然吸引着全球的科技人才；在美国主要的人工智能会议上发表论文的AI专家中，有三分之二来自外国。2019年，中国工程师占据了该精选群体的27%。许多中国人工智能专家在去美国学习或工作后将专业知识带回了祖国。新冠疫情和中美紧张局势导致这些数字逐渐减少。2022年上半年，美国向中国学生授予的签证数量只有2019年同期的一半。

  2. Centre for the Governance of AI: 人工智能治理中心，是英国的一个智库，致力于研究人工智能技术的应用和影响。
  3. Nvidia: 美国芯片设计公司，主要生产面向游戏、人工智能和数据中心等领域的高性能处理器和显卡。
  4. processing power: 处理能力，指计算机或其他电子设备执行各种操作的能力。
  5. SMIC: 中芯国际，是中国大陆最大的芯片制造商之一。
  6. TSMC: 台积电，是台湾的一家半导体制造公司，也是全球最大的代工厂之一。
  7. know-how: 专业知识、技术秘诀，指的是某个行业或领域内的实践经验、技能和技术知识。
  8. boffins: 科学家、专家，是对科研人员的俚语称呼。
  9. dwindle: 减少，缩小。
  10. “churning out”意为大量生产、连续不断地制造。例如：The factory has been churning out cars at a remarkable pace.（这家工厂一直以惊人的速度大量生产汽车。）
  11. “having trouble getting their hands on”是一个习语，意为遇到困难，难以获得或接触到某物。例如：The company is having trouble getting their hands on the raw materials they need.（这家公司在获取所需原材料时遇到了困难。）
The triple shortage—of data, hardware and expertise—has been a hurdle for China. Whether it will hold Chinese AI ambitions back much longer is another matter.
Take data. In February local authorities in Beijing, where nearly a third of China’s AI firms are located, promised to release data from 115 state- affiliated organisations, giving model-builders 15,880 data sets to play with. The central government has previously signalled it wants to dismantle Chinese apps’ walled gardens, potentially liberating more data, says Kayla Blomquist, an American former diplomat in China now at Oxford University. The latest models are also able to transfer their machine learnings from one language to another. OpenAI says that GPT-4 performs remarkably well on tasks in Chinese despite scarce Chinese source material in its training data. Baidu’s ERNIE was trained on lots of English-language data, notes Jeffrey Ding of George Washington University.
In hardware, too, China is finding workarounds. The Financial Times reported in March that SenseTime, which is blacklisted by America, was using middlemen to skirt the export controls. Some Chinese AI firms are harnessing Nvidia’s processors through cloud servers in other countries. Alternatively, they can buy more of Nvidia’s less advanced wares—to keep serving the vast Chinese market, Nvidia has designed sanctions-compliant ones that are between 10% and 30% slower than top-of-the-range kit. These end up being costlier for the Chinese customers per unit of processing power. But they do the job.
数据、硬件和专业知识的三重短缺一直是中国发展人工智能面临的障碍。它是否会继续阻碍中国的人工智能雄心壮志，则是另外一个问题。
就数据而言，北京市几乎三分之一的中国人工智能公司于2月承诺公开来自115个国有机构的数据，使得模型构建者可以使用15880组数据进行实验。美国前驻华外交官Kayla Blomquist现在在牛津大学任教，她表示，中央政府此前已经表明希望拆除中国应用程序的围墙花园，从而有可能释放更多数据。最新的模型还可以将其机器学习从一种语言转移到另一种语言。OpenAI表示，尽管训练数据中稀缺中文来源材料，但GPT-4在中文任务上表现出色。乔治·华盛顿大学的Jeffrey Ding指出，百度的ERNIE是通过大量的英文数据进行训练的。

China could partly alleviate the dearth of chips—and of brain power—with the help of “open-source” models. Such models’ inner workings can be downloaded by anyone and fine-tuned to a specific task. Those include the numbers, called “weights”, which define the structure of the model and which are derived from costly training runs. Researchers at Stanford used the weights from LLaMA, Meta’s foundation model, to build one called Alpaca for less than $600, compared with perhaps $100m for training something like GPT-4. Alpaca performs just as well as the original version of ChatGPT on some tasks.
Chinese AI labs could similarly avail themselves of open-source models, which embody the collective wisdom of international research teams. Matt Sheehan of the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace, another think-tank, says that China has form in being a “fast follower”—its labs have absorbed advances from abroad and rapidly incorporated them into their own models, often with flush state resources. A prominent Silicon Valley venture capitalist is more blunt, calling open-source models a gift to the*.
Such considerations make it hard to imagine that either America or China could build an unbridgeable lead in AI modelling. Each may well end up with AIs of similar ability, even if it costs China over the odds in the face of American sanctions. But if the race of the model-builders is a dead heat, America has one thing going for it that may make it the big AI winner—its ability to spread cutting-edge innovation throughout the economy.
中国可以在“开源”模型的帮助下部分缓解芯片和人才短缺的问题。这些模型的内部结构可以被任何人下载并调整到特定任务上使用。这包括称为“权值”的数字，这些数字定义了模型的结构，并来自昂贵的训练运行。斯坦福大学的研究人员利用Meta公司的基础模型LLaMA的权重，仅花费不到600美元就建立了一个名为Alpaca的模型，相比之下GPT-4的培训成本可能达到1亿美元左右。Alpaca在某些任务上与ChatGPT的原始版本表现一样好。
中国的人工智能实验室也可以通过采用开源模型来获益，这些模型体现了国际研究团队的集体智慧。卡内基国际和平基金会的另一位智库专家Matt Sheehan表示，中国在成为“快速跟随者”方面有着丰富的经验，其实验室已经吸收了国外的先进技术，并迅速将其纳入自己的模型中，通常是在政府的资金支持下进行。一位知名的硅谷风险投资家更直言不讳地表示，开源模型是送给*的礼物。
这些因素使得很难想象美国或中国在人工智能建模上可以建立一个无法跨越的领先优势。即使面对美国的制裁，中国也可能会花费过多成本，最终拥有与美国相似的人工智能能力。但如果模型构建者的竞赛是胶着状态，那么美国有一件事情让它成为大人物——即将尖端创新技术传播到经济中。

Its fintech platforms, 5G telecoms and high-speed rail are all world-class. Still, those successes may be the exception, not the rule, says Mr Ding. Particularly, China has done less well in deploying cloud computing and business software—both complementary to AI.
And though American export controls may not derail all Chinese model-building, they constrain China’s tech industry more broadly, thereby slowing the adoption of new technology. Moreover, Chinese businesses as a whole, and especially small and medium-sized ones, are short of technologists who act as conduits for technological diffusion. Swathes of the economy are dominated by state-owned firms, which tend to be stodgy and change-averse. Parts of it are dodgy. China’s “Big Fund” for chips, which raised $50bn in 2014 with a view to backing domestic semiconductor firms, has been mired in scandals. Many of the thousands of new AI startups are AI in name only, slapping on the label to get a slice of the lavish subsidies doled out by the state to the favoured industry.
As a consequence, China’s private sector may struggle to take full advantage of generative AI, especially if the * imposes strict rules to prevent chatbots from saying something its censors dislike. The handicaps would come on top of Mr *’s broader suborning of private enterprise, including a two-and-a-half-year crackdown on China’s tech industry.
Although the anti-tech campaign has officially ended, it has left deep scars, not least in the AI business. Last year private investments in Chinese AI startups amounted to $13.5bn, less than one-third of the sum that flowed to their American rivals. In the first four months of 2023 the funding gap appears only to have widened, according to PitchBook, a data provider. Whether or not generative AI proves revolutionary, the free market has placed its bet on who will make the most of it. ■
中国金融科技平台、5G电信和高速铁路等都是世界级的。不过，丁先生表示，这些成功或许只是例外而非常态。特别是，在部署云计算和企业软件方面，中国做得不如人意——这两者都是人工智能的重要补充。
尽管美国的出口管制可能不会破坏中国所有的模型构建，但它们会更广泛地限制中国的科技行业，从而减缓新技术的采纳。此外，整个中国的企业，特别是中小企业，缺乏作为技术传播渠道的技术人才。经济的大部分领域被国有企业所主导，它们往往保守且不喜变革。其中一些领域还存在问题。中国为芯片设立的“大基金”曾于2014年筹集了500亿美元，旨在支持国内半导体企业，但后来陷入丑闻。成千上万的新型人工智能初创公司仅仅打上人工智能的标签以获取政府给予优惠产业的慷慨补贴。
